Sample email message

Here's a sample email message I sent to myself via GMail. It has a few less header lines than if I'd sent it from one service provider to another, but this has all of the bits that most email messages have. The message body contains two copies of the message (one in plain text, one in HTML for the mail clients that can display it) and a Base64-encoded version of the Infobits logo. Unsurprisingly, the longest part is the encoded logo. If you're using a screen reader, it may take tens of minutes to hear the whole attachment -- it's around 450 lines long, with 80 characters per line! I don't recommend it unless you're really bored.

The lines that begin with two dashes and end with 28 letters and numbers are the MIME boundaries and mark the beginnings and ends of various parts of the message. You can see how the plain text and HTML versions of the email message don't have suggested filenames but the logo PNG (a type of image) file does.
